Sir Alex Ferguson dies aged 83
Published: 31 May 2025

Football fans across the globe are mourning the loss of one of the greatest managers in the history of the sport. Sir Alex Ferguson, the legendary former manager of Manchester United, has reportedly passed away peacefully at the age of 83.

According to family sources, the cause of death was complications related to a recurring heart condition, for which he had previously undergone surgery in 2018. Despite ongoing health challenges, Sir Alex had remained in good spirits and closely followed football until his final days.

Known for his fiery passion, tactical genius, and the infamous "hairdryer treatment", Ferguson led Manchester United through an era of unmatched dominance. Appointed in 1986, he remained at the helm for over 26 years, collecting a staggering 38 trophies, including 13 Premier League titles and 2 UEFA Champions League crowns.

Sir Alex’s influence went beyond the pitch. He nurtured talents like Ryan Giggs, Paul Scholes, Cristiano Ronaldo, and David Beckham, fostering not just footballers but leaders. His leadership style, fierce competitiveness, and ability to evolve with the modern game earned him widespread admiration, even among rivals.

Tributes have poured in from across the footballing world. Cristiano Ronaldo tweeted, "He was more than a manager to me. A mentor, a father figure, a legend. Rest in peace, boss."

Manchester United has announced plans for a full public tribute to be held at Old Trafford this weekend. The Premier League confirmed a minute’s silence will be observed at all fixtures, and players will wear black armbands in his honour.

Sir Alex is survived by his wife, Cathy, their three sons, and several grandchildren. He leaves behind an unmatched legacy and a football world forever changed by his presence.
